Iris Presentation Attack Detection

During my CSE Summer Enrichment Internship in Computer Vision at Notre Dame (May - Aug 2024), I developed a Python program to efficiently curate 2,651 iris images from a large dataset, automating the creation of a labeled PDF. I also automated the IriTech Iris sensor using a Python script, streamlining the capture and labeling of iris images. My work included structuring the dataset for balanced training and implementing a ResNet-18 model for iris Presentation Attack Detection (PAD), achieving flawless detection performance. Additionally, I regularly presented my progress, collaborated across departments, and showcased my project in a research poster at the University of Notre Dame Summer Undergraduate Research Symposium.

ND Data Club: Kroger Data Analysis

I collaborated with club members to analyze 10 years of Kroger data, identifying top and bottom-performing products across locations and presenting our findings in Chicago. I also created a strategic project roadmap, focusing on key deliverables, and produced clear data visualizations using Python and Excel.
