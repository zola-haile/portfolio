In a collaborative project focused on Olympic data analysis, I worked with a team to select a compelling topic and gather extensive data, specifically from the "120 Years of Olympic History: Athletes and Results" dataset by Griffin, R. (2018), retrieved from Kaggle. Our data preprocessing efforts using MATLAB involved reducing the number of rows from 271,116 to 39,732, while maintaining the 15 columns, ensuring a manageable and relevant dataset.

With the cleaned data, we designed and developed a custom application using MATLAB App Designer, tailored to meet specific objectives: displaying the number of gold medals won by each country, identifying the athlete with the most medals in each sport, providing a breakdown of the types of medals won by each country, and exploring the relationship between age and the chances of winning a medal.

This application is particularly useful for sports betters, coaches, trainers, and aspiring Olympic athletes, offering them valuable insights and tools for performance analysis and strategic decision-making.
